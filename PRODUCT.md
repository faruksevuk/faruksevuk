# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Startup founders, CTOs, and hiring managers at European tech companies (mostly Germany/NL/Nordics), arriving from a cold outreach email or Faruk's email signature. Context: at their desk between meetings, skeptical, 10–15 seconds of attention. Job to be done: decide "is this 21-year-old worth a reply or a call?" Secondary users: recruiters and hackathon/university contacts checking him out.

## Product Purpose

Faruk Sevük's personal site (faruksevuk.com). It is the landing page of his outreach funnel: every cold email links here. Success = a visitor leaves convinced he ships real products (not tutorials), and either books a call, replies to the email, or downloads the resume. The site must prove, fast: Meld is live on the App Store (solo-built), his automation cut a real firm's manual work by 70%, he won a hackathon, and he's easy to reach.

## Brand Personality

Direct, warm, confident — "messaging a senior colleague, not writing a cover letter." Young and fearless but precise; a builder who talks in shipped outcomes and numbers, not adjectives. A small streak of playfulness (he hides easter eggs in his site) that never costs credibility.

## Anti-references

- The previous pirate-costume theme (Griffy/Pirata One fonts, gold-on-wood, ⚓ cursor, blurred portrait): personality drowned the professional signal.
- Generic AI-generated portfolio slop: hero-metric templates, identical card grids, tiny uppercase eyebrows over every section, gradient text.
- Corporate-speak: "passionate", "innovative", "cutting-edge", "I would love to explore opportunities" — banned in copy, mirroring his email voice rules.
- Template minimalism with no point of view (beige Notion-clone with Inter).

## Design Principles

1. **Proof over polish-talk.** Every claim on the page is a shipped artifact, a number, or a link that resolves (App Store, GitHub, resume, calendar).
2. **The 10-second skim wins.** A skeptical founder must get who/what/why-care above the fold; depth is for the second visit.
3. **Personality as seasoning, not costume.** One memorable playful touch (easter egg), zero theme cosplay; the face is visible, never blurred.
4. **Frictionless next step.** Book-a-call and email are one click from everywhere; the site is a conversion surface, not a museum.
5. **Practice what you preach.** He sells himself as someone who ships quality software — the site itself must be fast, responsive, accessible, zero-build vanilla HTML/CSS.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: ≥4.5:1 body contrast, visible focus states, semantic landmarks, alt text on real images, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all animation, no cursor hijacking, touch targets ≥44px. Site must work at 360px width and on slow connections (it's opened from email on phones).
